# Plan: Study Clean Architecture Book

A structured 6-8 week study plan to master software architecture principles, SOLID design, component design, and clean architecture patterns through Robert C. Martin's seminal work, with practical application to your existing C# projects.

## Steps

1. **Foundation (Week 1-2)**: Read Part I (Introduction) and Part II (Programming Paradigms) to understand what architecture is and the three paradigms (structured, OO, functional) that inform architectural decisions.

2. **SOLID Principles (Week 2-3)**: Study Part III (Design Principles) covering SRP, OCP, LSP, ISP, and DIP; apply each principle by refactoring code in your [CSharp](CSharp) practice projects.

3. **Component Architecture (Week 4-5)**: Work through Part IV (Component Principles) on component cohesion and coupling; analyze components in your [DesignPatterns](DesignPatterns) and [AspNet/osiModel](AspNet/osiModel) projects.

4. **Core Architecture (Week 5-6)**: Study Part V (Architecture) including dependency rules, boundaries, and the clean architecture model; diagram and evaluate architecture of [DotNet](DotNet) projects.

5. **Implementation Details (Week 7)**: Read Part VI (Details) on databases, frameworks, and the web as implementation details; identify improvements in your [AspNet](AspNet) web projects.

6. **Case Studies & Practice (Week 8)**: Complete Part VII case studies; refactor one existing project (suggest [AspNet/osiModel](AspNet/osiModel)) applying clean architecture principles learned.

## Further Considerations

1. **Pace adjustment**: This is ~30 pages/day pace. Adjust based on your schedule: faster (4 weeks intensive) or slower (12 weeks relaxed)?

2. **Hands-on emphasis**: Should the plan include specific refactoring exercises in your C# projects after each section, or focus primarily on reading comprehension?

3. **Note-taking approach**: Create summaries in [Plans](Plans) folder, or maintain notes alongside your [CSharp/LanguageReference.txt](CSharp/LanguageReference.txt) style documentation?
